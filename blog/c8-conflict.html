What not to do when in conflict

06/19/15

I have to handle conflict Every.Single.Day. I mean it. Some days I am successful than others :) In attempting to analyze what makes a conflict happen or what nips it in the bud, I've noticed a few case behaviors that the people in conflict tend to do. I will share my observations with you, along with possible fixes:

  • Passive-aggressiveness: one might be aware that he/she is doing it, or it might just be a way he/she responds (not a good way, but his/her way). Could look something like:
    • pretend to not hear what other is saying, doing
    • making noises to cover the other (bang stuff on desk/table, croon, whistle, put on headphones)
    • say stuff that passive aggressive people say. Eg: Fine, whatever or I'm not mad

    Possible fixes:

    • Start paying attention to your own passive-aggressive behaviors as well as your peers and resolve to choose a better way to handle. Some really good tips are in this article.
    • Journaling is your friend. Observing and jotting down the passive aggressive incidents can help with identifying a pattern and adopting an appropriate fix going forward.
  • Pound the ohter party with questions and/or requests when other is visibly bothered. Best would be to drop whatever the issue for the moment. Get back at a later and less heated moment
  • Issue strong accusations. Eg You are wrong. Instead rephrase with I statements like I feel judged when you say that
  • Throw words like always and never in our statements. It is probably not true and it only serves the purpose escalate the conflict. Stick with referring to the present situation only.
  • Say stuff like I don't want to or Cause I say so. Rather, take a deep breath and try to give one best reason why you (don't) want a particular thing.
  • Most dangerous behavior that can occur is letting the conflict escalate to yelling and/or physical aggression. Conflict resolution theory advises to use assertiveness instead of aggressiveness

What not to do when in conflict

06/19/15

Tweet
B. conflict: what not to do - be a passive-aggressive beach (applies equally to men and women): - pretend to not hear what other is saying, doing - making noises to cover the other (bang stuff on desk/table, croon, whistle, put on headphones) - https://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/passive-aggressive-diaries/201011/10-things-passive-aggressive-people-say - http://www.wikihow.com/Stop-Being-Passive-Aggressive - fixes: - having a journal is your friend: you can have a journal of happiness (- 3 gratitudes, positive journaling - from https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fLJsdqxnZb0). You can also have a journal of your behavior - be assertive not aggressive - http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2014/06/26/stop-being-passive-aggressive-behavior-signs-_n_5515877.html - pound with questions and/or requests when other is visibly bothered; drop it for now. Get back at a later and more opportune moment - yell - get mad - throw: "You are wrong". Instead rephrase with "I feel judged when you say that". - include "always", "never". It is probably not true and it only makes your discustion partner more angry - use "I don't want to", "Cause I say so". Rather take a deep breath and try to give one best reason why you (don't) want a particular thing
